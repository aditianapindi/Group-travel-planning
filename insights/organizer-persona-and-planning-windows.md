# Insight: The Serial Organizer & Planning Windows

## Core Finding
The organizer role is fixed, gendered, and stressful — and planning windows vary dramatically by trip type. These two patterns together define when and who to target.

---

## The Serial Organizer

**The role doesn't rotate.** Academic research and qualitative evidence consistently show that the same person plans every time. They self-select by capability (tech-savvy, detail-oriented) and the group implicitly accepts it. [Source: Fardous et al., 2019; ScienceDirect, 2021]

**They likely plan for 2-3 distinct groups/year** — family vacation + friend trip + possibly a milestone event. No published survey confirms this frequency, but trip data (avg 2-3 trips/year) combined with role fixedness makes it a reasonable inference. **Must validate in interviews.**

**The burden is real and measurable:**
- 71% of arrangers find it stressful [Source: CivicScience, 2024]
- 10+ hours/trip on admin [Source: SquadTrip — directional]
- They front costs on personal cards
- Longer planning = more stress (counter-intuitive) [Source: CivicScience, 2024]

**Implication:** Target the organizer, not the group. They're the one searching for solutions. If we reduce their 10 hours to 1, they'll pull the group onto the platform.

---

## Planning Windows

| Trip Type | When Planning Starts | Product Value |
|-----------|---------------------|---------------|
| Weekend getaway | 1-7 days | Low — too fast for coordination |
| Domestic family | 4-8 weeks | Medium |
| International group | 3-6 months | **Highest** |
| Festival travel (India) | Should be 2-3 months, actually <1 week | **Wedge: nudge opportunity** |
| Milestone (bach) | 4-6 months | **High** |

**India-specific gap:** 46% of domestic flights booked <1 week before travel [Source: MakeMyTrip via Skift, 2023]. Festival travel should be planned 2-3 months ahead (trains sell out 60-90 days in advance) but behavior is last-minute. Cleartrip already validates the nudge approach with their Long Weekend Tracker.

---

## What This Means for the Product

1. **Acquisition timing**: International and milestone trips are where users need the product most and have the longest engagement window. Festival travel in India is a wedge — low effort nudge feature, high perceived value.

2. **Retention problem reframed**: With 2-3 trips/year across different groups, the challenge isn't seasonal demand — it's staying on the organizer's phone between trips. The organizer is the link. If we earn their trust on trip 1, they bring us to the next group.

3. **Interview gaps to close**: No published data on cross-group organizing frequency or what triggers planning. Added to interview guide (Q11 expanded, Q13 new).
