## Rahul's Interviews

### Interviewee 7 (Female, 33, IT, Bangalore, Kerala background)
**Role in trips:** Contributor (not organizer)
**Trips discussed:** 2

#### Trip 1 — Girls Gang, India (Kashmir)
**Group:** 8 members total; 5 typically travel; 4 are the core "always-make-it" group

**Why 5 vs 8:** Dates don't align, other commitments — standard dropout reasons

**How they decide WHERE:**
- Seasonality + destination together (e.g., monsoon = Kerala, summer = Himalayas)
- This trip: Himalayas. Options: Kashmir, Himachal Pradesh, Meghalaya
- Meghalaya dropped — one had already been. Spiti done before. Kashmir won by elimination.
- "Everyone was convinced, no arguments there."

**How they decide WHEN:**
- Leader does groundwork — checks who is available on which dates
- Picks most common dates → rest are expected to oblige
- Everyone does stick to it

**Booking behavior:**
- Book travel tickets first — intentional lock-in mechanism
- Quote: *"We already paid for tickets, no backing off now"*

**Planning process:**
- Leader + contributors split tasks: hotels, activities, places to visit, food
- Leader tracks everyone meticulously for updates
- Communication: WhatsApp — messages get missed in flow but someone brings it up again
- Expenses: Splitwise, entered every evening at the hotel
- Post-trip: balances settled within a week

**Tools used:**
- Flights: Skyscanner, Cleartrip
- Accommodation: Airbnb, Hostelr
- Research: TripAdvisor, Google Maps
- Itinerary: ChatGPT / AI tools — *"exactly how we want, more value in less time/money"*

**Planning timeline:** Max 2 weeks. Move fast to maintain momentum.

**Budget:** Rough upfront estimate. Typically works for 5-6 of the 8.

**Execution quality:** 95%+ plan match. Misses are external (traffic, weather), not planning failures.

**Key insight — peer knowledge gap:**
- Went over budget on taxis. Cheaper modes existed but weren't on any website.
- Quote: *"That info was nowhere. After talking to people who already went there, then we knew."*
- Expressed desire: talk to people who've been there — not browse travel sites

**On AI tools:**
- Quote: *"ChatGPT helped for latest itinerary, it was much relaxation. I would look at it as AID, helper — but we won't let it take away the fun."*

**On trip planners:**
- Quote: *"We don't approach trip planners. There's so much in planning and talking among us, that's a memory."*

#### Trip 2 — International, Austria (6 couples, 12 people)

**How it formed:** Interviewee 7 + husband had Austria on their list. Mentioned it casually → friends said they'd join → became 6 couples.

**Planner:** Husband — sole planner and organizer. Created full PPT, Excel sheets. Did NOT consult others.
**Interviewee 7:** Contributor.
**Rest of 5 couples:** Fully passive.

**Planning timeline:** 2-3 weeks (after visas cleared)

**The plan reveal:** Full presentation — itinerary, budgets, places, everything in detail.

**What broke:**
- Larger group = people-as-people problem
- Some couple took a casual walk, came back late → missed an activity
- Some couples: "Why go elsewhere, let's stay here longer" → heated discussions on the trip
- Quote: *"It did affect the mood. It was not okay."*
- Plan match: **70%**

**Root cause:**
- *"We set the expectations. We thought they listened and understood the itinerary. Seems they didn't."*

**Final take:**
- Quote: *"Group travel — I would prefer to travel only with like-minded people after all these experiences. Setting expectations prior will help."*
- **The 5 couples from Austria — Interviewee 7 doesn't travel with them anymore. They're still friends. But will never travel together again.**

#### Key Signals
| Theme | Signal |
|---|---|
| Peer knowledge > browse | Explicitly wanted real talk, not search |
| AI as aid, not replacement | Comfortable with AI but protective of the social planning process |
| Organizer burden | Leader does all the coordination tracking |
| Commitment device | Book tickets early to lock people in |
| Like-mindedness = execution quality | 95% match (close friends) vs 70% (larger mixed group) |
| Expectation gap at scale | "We thought they listened" — the bigger the group, the bigger the gap |

### Interviewee 3 (Male, 32, Hyderabad, Travel Enthusiast)
**Role in trips:** Active planner / contributor
**Trips discussed:** 1 (Lonavala/Mahabaleshwar, group of 12)

#### Trip — Lonavala / Mahabaleshwar, 12 people (mixed friends, M+F)

**How dates were fixed:** 2 friends had birthdays back-to-back — dates were fixed first, not the destination.

**How they chose WHERE:**
- Monsoon season + Western Ghats = constraint
- Options: Lonavala, Coorg, Wayanad, Manali (Himachal)
- International also considered but budget ruled it out at 12 people
- Manali dropped: landslides happening
- Wayanad: better food options (food was a key decision factor)
- Lonavala won: **accessibility + connectivity** (easy from Mumbai) + enough food options

**Decision-making process:**
- Lots of difference of opinion even after deciding the place
- Quote: *"We ran polls and took the democratic way"*
- Core tension: spend same money moving around vs. one place, full experience
- Lonavala fit: one base, big house with kitchen + pool

**Accommodation:** Airbnb-style platform. Found a property with kitchen, pool. Booked it.

**Group dynamics:**
- 12 people: 7 passive, 2-3 actively planning
- Communication: WhatsApp (primary)
- Expenses: Mix of personal notes, WhatsApp posts, some Splitwise — reconciled later

**Splitwise pain point:**
- Teetotallers don't drink — alcohol expense shouldn't apply to them
- Vegetarians don't eat non-veg — same
- Quote: *"You have to select who the cost applies to — that's a pain. So we said we'll do it later."*
- Deferred expense entry → reconciliation burden at end

**Budget:** Discussed upfront, some constraints

**Momentum disruption:** Air India crash news → people scared, PTSD moment for the group

**On large group travel:**
- Quote: *"In a trip of 8+ people, if everyone is saying everything is perfect — that's too good to be true. You'd need all of them to be like-minded for that."*
- Insight: similar-minded people reduce variability, but don't eliminate it

**Execution quality:**
- Plan match: **80-85%**
- Miss: Food. Reviews said good — they didn't like it on arrival.
- Monsoon: food delivery delayed. Had to pre-order 2 hours ahead.
- Quote: *"We trusted the review / property. It wasn't what we expected."*

#### Key Signals
| Theme | Signal |
|---|---|
| Date-first planning | Dates locked by social occasion, destination chosen around them |
| Democratic decision-making | Polls for large group — but still messy |
| Expense complexity | Dietary / alcohol splits make Splitwise painful in practice |
| Deferred reconciliation | "Do it later" is the default — creates end-of-trip debt |
| Trust in reviews → disappointment | Food was a decision factor, and reviews failed them |
| Like-mindedness at scale | More people = more variability, even with planning |
| Food as trip factor | Came up multiple times — food options drive destination choice AND execution quality |

## Cross-Cutting Themes (Akanksha's Batch)

1. **Date & destination alignment is #1 friction** — confirmed across nearly all interviews. Different budgets, objectives, leave schedules.
2. **Passive participants cause financial loss** — not just frustration. Advance payments, no refunds, cost redistribution.
3. **Scattered communication** — WhatsApp groups + DMs + calls = decisions get lost. Varun explicitly wants "single container."
4. **Agents and reviews are untrustworthy** — agents optimize for themselves (3 separate interviews confirm). Reviews are fake/AI-generated. Reality doesn't match.
5. **Budget management** — splitting, tracking, dropout refunds. Splitwise breaks at scale (15+ people).
6. **Local logistics gaps** — public transport visibility, language barriers, real-time route/weather.
7. **Tool fragmentation** — Google Maps, Skyscanner, MakeMyTrip, TripAdvisor, Instagram, YouTube, ChatGPT, Splitwise, WhatsApp. No unified platform.
8. **"Nodes" drive trips** — Sabya's insight: core knit group with 1-2 initiators who activate interest. This IS the organizer acquisition model.
9. **Budget = destination filter** — Yash: ₹70k eliminated 5 of 7. Ashik: place and budget are interlinked. Eswar: budget constrains everything.
10. **Once dates + destination lock, rest is easy** — Omkar's insight confirmed by multiple interviews.

## Proposed App Feature List (from team's synthesis)

### AI Itinerary
- Builds from flight times and hotel
- Voice input to add places
- One tap to add local suggestions
- Auto language translation
- Everyone views and comments

### Collaboration
- Invite group
- Add, edit, comment on any activity
- Suggest changes

### Decisions
- Vote with hard deadline
- Auto closes, organiser decides

### Budget
- Anonymous input from each member
- Organiser sees range only

### Expenses
- One tap logging
- No transaction limit
- Auto settlement
- Payment nudges via text/WhatsApp

### Time Management
- Schedule visible to all
- 1 hour activity reminder
- Nudge for late members

### Organiser Dashboard
- Confirmed, paid, unresolved

### Passive Member
- One tap to confirm, vote, pay
