## The Group Travel Problem

When 4+ people travel together, it's no longer a booking problem — it's a **coordination problem**.

### Five Dimensions of Coordination
1. Collective decision-making (where do we go?)
2. Calendar coordination (when can everyone go?)
3. Financial alignment (what can everyone afford?)
4. Itinerary collaboration (what do we do each day?)
5. Real-time communication (who's doing what right now?)

### Current Tool Landscape

| Tool | Solves | Misses |
|------|--------|--------|
| WhatsApp Groups | Discussion, link sharing | No structure, messages buried, polls expire |
| Google Sheets | Budget tracking, lists | No mobile-first UX, intimidating for non-planners |
| Splitwise | Expense splitting post-trip | No planning, no itinerary, only useful after trip |
| Wanderlog | Itinerary + map, some collab | Weak group voting, no budget splitting |
| TripIt | Flight/hotel org, alerts | No collaborative editing, single-user focused |
| Google Travel | Inspiration, saved places | No group features at all |
| MakeMyTrip/Booking | Search and book | Individual only, no group coordination |

**Key insight**: Travelers need 4 separate apps (Wanderlog + Splitwise + Google Maps + WhatsApp) for a single trip.

## Key Planning Challenges

### 1. The Decision Bottleneck
- Every group trip needs collective decisions on destination, dates, budget, activities
- Each decision handled in different tools → context-switching kills momentum
- Larger groups plan months in advance but no tool supports long multi-stakeholder decision cycles

### 2. The Organizer Tax
- One person absorbs all cognitive load: logistics, coordination, conflict resolution, budgeting
- This labor is invisible and unrewarded
- Organizer often has the worst trip experience
- Mixed groups (Gen ACE adventurers + Gen LUX luxury seekers) make organizer's job harder

### 3. Unspoken Budget Misalignment
- #1 source of group trip friction
- Most groups never have explicit budget conversations
- India domestic: typically <₹50K/person; international: >₹1L/person
- Gap between Tier I and Tier II city spending expectations within same friend group

### 4. Passive Participation
- In groups of 6+, 2-3 members are passive (don't vote, don't respond)
- Not because they don't care — tools require too much effort
- 70%+ planning on mobile in 2-3 minute windows
- Any interaction >30 seconds loses passive members

### 5. No Single Source of Truth
- Info scattered: WhatsApp, Google Docs, booking emails, Instagram saves, Notes app, Splitwise
- No single place shows: where, when, daily plan, cost per person, and what I need to do

### 6. The Momentum Problem
- Energy curve: High excitement → logistics decline → 2-3 week death zone → emergency sprint → messy settlement
- No product maintains momentum through this curve

## The Typical Group Trip Journey

1. "Let's go somewhere" in WhatsApp
2. 12 destinations suggested across 3 conversations
3. WhatsApp poll: 3/7 vote, poll expires unresolved
4. Dates debated, someone can't make it, sub-conversations splinter
5. Budget never discussed explicitly — ₹5K vs ₹15K/day expectations
6. One person makes Google Sheet, builds itinerary alone
7. Rush booking: double-booked rooms, sold-out trains, spiked prices
8. During trip: organizer = GPS + concierge + accountant
9. Post-trip: messy Splitwise, incomplete entries, unpaid debts

## AI Disruption Opportunity

### AI-Powered Tasks
- Destination research & comparison
- Itinerary generation by group size, budget, preferences
- Real-time price tracking & deal alerts
- Expense categorization & split calculations
- Route optimization for multi-stop trips

### Industry Moves
- MakeMyTrip: GenAI assistant (Oct 2024) — personalized itineraries, multi-lingual
- Wanderlog: AI suggestions, route optimization, interactive maps
- ixigo: Real-time fare prediction, dynamic pricing alerts
- TravelPerk/TripActions: AI for corporate group travel

**Core AI opportunity**: Not just automation — reducing the coordination tax of group decisions. The hardest part isn't finding hotels; it's getting 6 people to agree.

## Problem Statement

Groups of friends and families struggle to plan trips together due to fragmented tools, unequal planning burden, unspoken budget conflicts, and passive participation — leading to exhausted organizers, suboptimal trips, and sometimes damaged relationships.
