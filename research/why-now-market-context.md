# Why Now — Market Context for Group Travel Planning

## Historical Evolution

### India: Pilgrimage → Agents → OTAs → WhatsApp → Gap
1. **Pilgrimage era** — Group travel in India began as religious: Char Dham yatras, temple circuits, family pilgrimages. Organized by community leaders or family elders. No tools needed — one person decided, everyone followed.
2. **Travel agent era** — As leisure travel grew (Goa, Manali, Kerala), travel agents handled everything. Thomas Cook, SOTC, Cox & Kings dominated. Agent = single point of contact for groups. Worked well but expensive, opaque pricing, zero customization.
3. **OTA disruption (2010s)** — MakeMyTrip, Goibibo, Cleartrip made individual booking cheap and transparent. But group coordination? Still zero support. OTAs optimized for search → book → confirm, not discuss → decide → align → book.
4. **WhatsApp era (2015-present)** — WhatsApp became India's de facto planning tool. Groups of 8-15 people plan trips in chat. Works for discussion, fails for decisions (polls expire, messages buried, no structure).
5. **The gap** — 2.5B domestic tourist visits in 2023, up 45% YoY [Source: India Ministry of Tourism via PIB, 2024], growing to 2.95B in 2024 [Source: Skift, 2024]. Monthly domestic travel searches grew from 103M to 141M between 2022-2024 [Source: Booking.com + Accenture, "How India Travels 2025" report — note: this is national search volume, not Tier II/III specific, though the same report confirms Tier II/III cities lead short-stay booking surges]. No tool bridges the WhatsApp-to-booking gap for groups. Holidify (10M+ monthly sessions) uses 3,000+ agents — still not peer-to-peer group planning.

### US: Reunions → Agents → OTAs → Fragmented Apps → Gap
1. **Reunion/road trip era** — Group travel = family reunions, road trips, spring break. AAA and travel agents handled logistics.
2. **OTA era (2000s)** — Expedia, Orbitz, Kayak made solo booking easy. Group? Still call the hotel.
3. **App fragmentation (2015-present)** — Wanderlog for itineraries, Splitwise for expenses, Google Sheets for planning, WhatsApp/iMessage for coordination. Average group trip = 4 separate apps. No dominant group-planning tool exists — Americans cobble together general-purpose tools [Source: editorial consensus across SquadTrip, Wandrly, travel planning guides].
4. **The gap** — US travel spending hit $1.335T in 2024 ($1.156T domestic + $178.9B inbound) [Source: US Travel Association, 2024]. 77% of Americans traveled with family/friends in 2025; only 11% solo [Source: Talker Research, n=2,000 frequent travelers, 2025]. 47% of Airbnb bookings are for groups of 3+ [Source: Airbnb/STR analysts, 2024-2025]. Yet multi-city fly-in trips, AirBnB/VRBO room coordination, and expense settlement remain unsolved as a unified experience. No app handles plan → decide → book → travel → settle.

## Why OTAs Failed at Group Travel

### The Commission Math Problem
- OTAs earn 15-25% commission on hotels, 3-5% on flights
- Group bookings = bulk negotiation = lower margins per booking
- Hotels resist OTA group rates because groups already negotiate directly
- Building group features is expensive; the revenue model doesn't support it

### Failed Group Features
- MakeMyTrip: GenAI assistant (Oct 2024) — personalized itineraries but still individual booking
- Booking.com: No group coordination layer at all
- OTAs optimize for **conversion** (individual → booking), not **consensus** (group → decision)
- Group travel requires 5+ decisions before anyone books anything — OTAs enter too late in the journey

### The Structural Mismatch
- OTA value prop: "Find the cheapest flight/hotel"
- Group travel value prop: "Get 6 people to agree on anything"
- These are fundamentally different problems. OTAs solve search. Groups need coordination.

## Travel Agent Resurgence

### US: The Comeback
- Travel advisors growing post-pandemic — complex trips, group coordination, accountability
- High-end segment: agents handle the organizer burden for a premium
- But inaccessible to casual groups (friends planning a weekend trip won't hire an agent)

### India: Never Left
- Agents never lost relevance — Holidify's model (3,000+ verified agents) proves demand
- Indian travel culture values relationship + trust + local knowledge
- Agents handle the "last mile" — train bookings, temple timings, festival coordination
- But agent model doesn't scale to the 141M monthly domestic travel searches [Source: Booking.com + Accenture, "How India Travels 2025"]

### The Insight
- Agents solve the organizer burden problem — by becoming the organizer
- An app must do the same thing at scale: absorb coordination work, not just display it

## Generational Shift

### Boomers: Pilgrimage & Package Tours
- Group travel = family obligation or religious duty
- Preferred: agent-handled, all-inclusive, predictable
- Budget: conservative, value-driven
- Planning: one decision-maker, others follow

### Millennials: Experience Seekers
- Group travel = friend groups, destination weddings, bachelor/bachelorette trips
- Preferred: curated but flexible, Instagram-worthy
- Budget: willing to spend but need transparency — 37% feel pressured to exceed budget on friend trips, 52% of millennials specifically [Source: Empower, May 2025, n=1,031 US adults]
- Planning: democratic but chaotic — everyone has opinions, no one coordinates
- 40% plan a trip with friends within the next year [Source: EF Ultimate Break/Qualtrics, Sept 2024, n=1,000+ US/Canada ages 18-35]
- 1 in 5 postponing home purchase to fund travel [Source: Empower, May 2025]

### Gen Z: Self-Discovery + Community
- Group travel = squad trips, festival travel (Sunburn, Coachella), workation groups
- Preferred: spontaneous, authentic, budget-conscious
- Budget: tight but creative (split everything, find deals) — 47% would go into debt (credit cards/BNPL) rather than skip travel [Source: EF Ultimate Break/Qualtrics, Sept 2024]
- 2x more likely to travel more in 2025 than other generations (23% vs 13%) [Source: Empower, May 2025]
- Planning: mobile-only, sessions ~1 min 10 sec [Source: Google micro-moments research], will abandon any tool that takes >30 seconds
- 65% of millennials/Gen Z use AI for travel planning (vs 29% Boomers) [Source: Empower, May 2025]

### The Multigenerational Tension
- Family trips increasingly span 3 generations (grandparents + parents + adult children)
- Each generation has different budget expectations, activity preferences, pace
- Boomers want structure; Gen Z wants flexibility — organizer mediates between both
- India: 82-84% choose destinations for cultural experiences [Source: Skyscanner Cultural Tourism Report, Aug 2025] — but the activity gap within families is huge
- This tension is **underserved** — no app designs for multigenerational groups
