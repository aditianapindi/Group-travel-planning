# User Pain Points — Secondary Research

Sources: Reddit, Quora, travel forums (Fodor's, TripAdvisor, Rick Steves), app reviews, travel blogs

**Source audit (March 2026):** Stats in this file have been verified against original reports. Verified stats are marked with [Source: ...]. Unverified stats are struck through with ~~strikethrough~~ and flagged. See bottom of file for full verification summary.

## Top 6 Pain Points (Ranked by Frequency)

### 1. Information Fragmentation (Mentioned most often)
- Trip details scattered across 5-10 apps: WhatsApp, Sheets, Docs, Gmail, Splitwise, Notes, Instagram saves
- "Flight options get shared but scrolling back requires archaeological excavation"
- Confirmed logistics buried under memes and off-topic chat
- Nobody knows what's confirmed vs. what's just an idea

### 2. Budget Conflict (Most emotionally charged)
- 59% of travelers report tension or arguments with travel partners [Source: AAA + Bread Financial Survey, Jan 2026, n=1,714 US adults — covers all ages, not just young travelers]
- "Over half" of Gen Z and millennials report money-related disagreements while traveling with friends; 35% of millennials cite "different budget ranges" as their top financial challenge; 1 in 5 have ended a friendship over a money issue [Source: Experian "Financial Stress of Traveling with Friends" survey, June 2025]
- ~~53% budget frustration~~ [UNVERIFIED — no original source found. The "over half" Experian finding is the closest real data point. Previous stat likely AI-generated.]
- "A trip fell apart over a $30 seafood dinner"
- Budget mismatches hidden due to social pressure → surprise costs → last-minute dropouts
- The conversation that NEEDS to happen first is the one everyone avoids

### 3. Decision Paralysis
- Average group trip: 50+ messages just to agree on dates
- Too many options + too many opinions + no structured framework = nothing gets decided
- ~~Anonymous voting gets 3.5x more honest responses~~ [UNVERIFIED — no study found with this multiplier. Research broadly supports anonymity encouraging candor, but one study found complete anonymity can *reduce* accuracy by decreasing accountability. Source: Journal of Experimental Social Psychology. Drop this stat.]
- **Multivoting** (each person gets multiple votes to distribute) makes groups 50% more likely to identify the correct option vs. plurality voting [Source: University of Washington, published in *Academy of Management Discoveries*, Sept 2022]. ⚠️ Notably, **ranked-choice voting performed poorly** in this study (only 32% success rate) — the original claim was misattributed to RCV.

### 4. Organizer Burnout
- One person absorbs all cognitive load: logistics, coordination, conflict, budget
- Labor is invisible and unrewarded
- Organizer often has the worst trip experience
- "Point person" workaround just concentrates stress instead of distributing it

### 5. Passive Members / Flaking
- "People don't give any input" — then complain about what was chosen
- Not laziness — tools require too much effort (scroll 200 messages to find a poll)
- 70% of travelers with smartphones do travel research on their phones [Source: Google/Think with Google, ~2016]; average smartphone session is ~1 min 10 sec and shrinking [Source: Google micro-moments research]. ⚠️ The "2-3 minute windows" claim is unverified — real sessions are even shorter.
- Once momentum dies, people quietly back out

### 6. Schedule Coordination
- "Incredibly stressful" across time zones and availabilities
- No simple visualization of "who can do what dates" across the group
- Cultural differences in punctuality add friction in diverse groups

## Behavioral Insights

### What actually breaks friendships:
- Money conversations avoided → resentment builds → erupts during trip
- Over-planning (100% scheduled) frustrates introverts — best trips: 80% planned, 20% spontaneous
- Group dynamics that work at home can collapse under travel stress

### What people actually build manually:
- Google Sheets with tabs: itinerary, expense splits, who-paid-what, change log
- Google Docs as "single source of truth" (but only organizer updates it)
- WhatsApp for real-time coordination (but everything gets buried)
- Splitwise for expenses (but no trip context)
- Google Forms for voting (but disconnected from everything else)

## India-Specific Insights

- Ideal group size: 8-15 people
- 82% of Indian travelers choose destinations primarily for cultural offerings (millennials: 84%, Gen Z: 80%) [Source: Skyscanner Cultural Tourism Report, Aug 2025, n=2,000 Indian adults ✅]
- Willingly adjust dates for festivals (Durga Puja, Holi, Onam)
- 85% of travelers prefer to book trips online [Source: Google-Kantar "Travel Rewired: Decoding the Indian Traveller," Nov 2025, n=1,000]. ~~41% plan 1-2 months ahead~~ [UNVERIFIED — not found in any source. Google-Kantar says "nearly 44% take more than a week to plan and book," which is a different claim. Drop or validate in interviews.]
- Safety (45%) tops considerations when choosing a cultural trip destination [Source: Skyscanner Cultural Tourism Report, Aug 2025 ✅ — note: not specifically "group" trips]
- 88% of Indian travelers consider sustainability important when choosing travel options [Source: Agoda Sustainable Travel Survey, 2026, n=1,036 across 8 Asian markets ✅]
- Theme-based travel surging: women-only, photography tours, LGBTQ+
- Many prefer locally-guided group tours over self-planning

## US-Specific Patterns

- Venmo (91.2M users) / Splitwise dominant for expense settling; Zelle processed $1T in 2024; P2P group payments grew 28% in 2025 [Source: PayPal/Zelle company data; CoinLaw]
- 58% of millennials/Gen Z split bills at least once a week; 29% split daily [Source: Forbes Advisor/OnePoll, Nov 2022, n=1,000 US P2P users]
- Budget differences (37%) and schedule coordination (31%) are top barriers to group travel [Source: Talker Research/Road Scholar, Feb 2026, n=2,000 ✅]
- Average person misses 2 trips/year because they can't find someone to go with; 1 in 8 struggle to get others on board [Source: Talker Research/Road Scholar, Feb 2026 ✅]
- Average traveler spends 16-18 hours researching, comparing, and booking; Gen Z/millennials spend 20+ hours [Source: Priceline/Harris Poll, Feb 2024; Go City, Oct 2024, n=2,000 ✅]
- Multi-city fly-in trips common (friends in different states) — 97% of bach/bachelorette parties are overnight/multi-day events; average spend $625 [Source: WeddingWire, 2023]
- Domestic road trips = Wanderlog's sweet spot
- 47% of Airbnb bookings are for groups of 3+ guests [Source: Airbnb/STR analysts, 2024-2025 ✅]; AirBnB/VRBO room coordination a major pain point
- 60% use search engines, 54% use OTA sites for planning [Source: Go City, Oct 2024, n=2,000]
- 44% worried about overspending; 51% pick destination based on price [Source: Go City, Oct 2024 ✅]

## Feature Requests Across All Sources (Ranked by Frequency)

1. Flexible pricing — don't charge $50 one-time for occasional travelers
2. Couples/family expense tracking — treat units as units, not individuals
3. Bedroom/room assignment system
4. Multi-currency handling with exchange rates
5. Integrated activity booking (stop making me leave the app)
6. Anonymous budget collection BEFORE planning starts
7. Preference collection system (dietary, activity, energy, budget)
8. Persistent information — important data retrievable, not buried in chat
9. Role-based access (organizer vs. participant permissions)
10. Travel style matching — surface mismatches before they become conflicts
