# Group Travel Planning — Interview Guide (Full)
**Duration:** 20-30 minutes | **Format:** Casual conversation (phone/video)
**41 questions — comprehensive version**

---

## Opening (2 min) — Build rapport, set context

1. Tell me about the last group trip you planned or were part of. How many people? Where did you go?

2. Were you the organizer, or was someone else driving it?

---

## The Journey (10 min) — Understand the actual process

3. Walk me through how the trip came together — from the first "let's go somewhere" to actually leaving. What happened step by step?

4. What tools did you use? WhatsApp, Google Sheets, anything else?

5. How long did it take from "let's do this" to having an actual plan? Did it ever stall? Where?

6. Was there a moment where the trip almost didn't happen? What caused that?

---

## Pain Points (8 min) — Dig into specific friction

7. What was the most frustrating part of the planning process?

8. How did you decide on the destination? How long did that take? Did everyone agree?

9. Did anyone say their budget out loud before planning started — or did you just figure it out as you went? What happened when someone's expectations didn't match?

10. Were there people in the group who didn't participate in planning? Why do you think that happened?

11. Who ended up doing most of the work? How did that feel for them?

---

## During & After Trip (5 min)

12. During the trip, was there a single place everyone could check for the plan? Or was it in someone's head?

13. How did you handle expenses during and after the trip? Was settling up smooth or messy?

---

## The Ideal (3 min) — Future state

14. If you could wave a magic wand, what's the one thing that would make group trip planning 10x easier?

15. If I built an app that did [their #1 pain point], would you move your group off WhatsApp to use it? What would it take — and what would make you quit it after one trip?

---

## India-Specific Probes (use with India interviewees)

16. Do you typically plan domestic trips (Goa, Manali, Kerala) or international? How does planning differ?

17. In your friend group, is there a budget gap? Like someone wants budget stays and another wants resorts — how do you handle that?

18. Do your parents or older family members participate in planning, or do they just show up?

---

## US-Specific Probes (use with US interviewees)

19. When you plan a trip with friends here, do you use any specific apps? Venmo/Splitwise for money?

20. Have you planned trips where people fly in from different cities? How did logistics coordination work?

21. Is there a difference in how you plan a domestic US trip vs. international trip with a group?

---

## Deep Dive Probes (use selectively based on conversation flow)

**Budget transparency**
22. Imagine before any planning started, everyone anonymously submitted their budget range and you could see the overlap. Would that have changed how your last trip went?

**Couples/families as units**
23. If you traveled as a couple or family unit — did expense tools treat you as one unit or two separate people? Was that annoying?

**Tool switching**
24. How many different apps or tools did you use for your last group trip? At what point did you stop using one and switch to another — what triggered the switch?

**Momentum death**
25. Was there a point where planning just... stalled? Like weeks of silence? What killed the momentum, and what (if anything) revived it?

**Room coordination**
26. How did you figure out who sleeps where — rooms, beds, Airbnb allocation? Was that smooth or a headache?

**Generational contrast**
27. If older family members were involved — how did their planning expectations differ from younger people? Who compromised?

**Agent comparison**
28. Have you ever used a travel agent for a group trip? What did they handle that you couldn't? What would have to be true for an app to replace that?

**Switch test**
29. If your organizer friend said "I'm using this new app for our next trip, join here" — would you click the link? What would make you say no?

---

## Business Viability Probes

**Category risk testing**
30. Have you ever downloaded an app specifically for trip planning? What happened — did you use it again for the next trip, or go back to WhatsApp/Sheets?

31. When you finished planning and were ready to book, where did you actually book? The same tool you planned in, or a separate site like Booking.com / MakeMyTrip?

32. How many group trips do you take in a year? Would you use a planning tool often enough to justify keeping it on your phone?

**Willingness to pay**
33. What travel apps or tools do you currently pay for? What made it worth paying?

34. If a group planning app was free for planning but charged for features you'd use during the trip — like offline access, live expense splitting, real-time itinerary updates — would that feel fair or annoying?

35. Would your group pay $3-5 total per trip for a tool that saved your organizer 10+ hours? Or does it need to be free?

**WhatsApp gravity**
36. Be honest — if someone in your group said "download this app," how many people would actually do it? What's the minimum it would need to do for people to bother?

37. What if the tool worked inside WhatsApp — like a bot or link you share in the group chat — instead of being a separate app? Would that change anything?

**Booking leakage**
38. If you could plan AND book flights/hotels inside the same tool, would you trust it? Or would you plan there and book on a site you already trust?

39. What would it take for you to enter your credit card into a new travel app? What makes you trust one booking site over another?

**Corrected assumptions**
40. When you're planning a trip on your phone, how long do you spend in one sitting? A quick 30 seconds, a few minutes, or longer?

41. If you had to vote on trip options, would you prefer: (a) everyone votes openly, (b) anonymous votes, or (c) each person distributes multiple votes across options?
